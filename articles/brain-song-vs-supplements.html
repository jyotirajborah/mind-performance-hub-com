How Cognitive Supplements Work

Nootropics and cognitive supplements work by directly altering brain chemistry through one or more mechanisms:

  • Neurotransmitter modulation — increasing dopamine, acetylcholine, or serotonin availability
  • Cerebral blood flow — increasing oxygen and nutrient delivery to brain tissue
  • Neuroprotection — reducing oxidative stress and inflammation
  • BDNF upregulation — some supplements like Lion's Mane have evidence for BDNF support
  • Adenosine receptor blockade — caffeine works this way to promote alertness

Common cognitive supplements include: Bacopa Monnieri, Lion's Mane mushroom, Rhodiola Rosea, Alpha-GPC, caffeine, L-theanine, and proprietary stacks like Alpha Brain or Qualia Mind.

How The Brain Song Works

The Brain Song works through brainwave entrainment — using rhythmic audio patterns to guide neural oscillations toward target frequency states. Unlike supplements:

  • It does not alter neurotransmitter levels directly
  • It influences the brain's electrical activity patterns, not its chemistry
  • Effects occur through neural synchronisation, not metabolic pathways
  • No absorption, no dosing, no tolerance development

The target is Gamma brainwave activity (30–100 Hz) — linked to focus, memory, and cognitive integration. Research suggests Gamma activity may also support BDNF production, creating an indirect biochemical benefit.

Who Should Choose What

The Brain Song suits you if:

  • You prefer non-chemical, non-invasive wellness tools
  • You are sensitive to stimulants or have medication interactions to consider
  • You want a one-time cost rather than a monthly supplement subscription
  • You are looking for a passive daily habit with no pills, powders, or timing

Supplements may suit you better if:

  • You need fast, acute cognitive effects (exam in 2 hours)
  • You have a specific deficiency (low Vitamin D, B12, iron) affecting cognition
  • You have a history of positive response to specific compounds
  • You prefer the flexibility of adjusting dose rather than a fixed audio routine
