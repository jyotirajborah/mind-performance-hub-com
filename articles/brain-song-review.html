The Science: BDNF and Gamma Brainwaves

Before assessing the product itself, it helps to understand the science it's built on.

What is BDNF? Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor is a protein that supports the survival of existing neurons and encourages the growth of new ones. It plays a key role in learning, memory consolidation, and cognitive flexibility. Higher BDNF levels are consistently associated with better memory, sharper focus, and resilience against cognitive decline.

Research has shown that various lifestyle factors — exercise, sleep, fasting, and meditation — all support BDNF production. The Brain Song draws on a more novel angle: the relationship between Gamma brainwave frequencies (30–100 Hz) and BDNF activity.

What are Gamma brainwaves? Gamma waves are the fastest brainwave frequencies and are associated with high-level cognitive processing, attention, and memory binding — the process by which the brain links different aspects of a memory together. Studies in both animal models and humans suggest a link between Gamma activity and neurotrophic support in the brain.

The idea of using audio to influence brainwave states isn't new — it falls under the broader field of brainwave entrainment, which includes techniques like binaural beats, isochronic tones, and auditory frequency following. While research in this area is still emerging, several peer-reviewed studies have shown measurable effects on brainwave activity from carefully designed audio stimuli.

Is the science settled? Not entirely. But the foundations the Brain Song draws on — BDNF's role in cognition and Gamma waves' link to cognitive performance — are well-supported areas of neuroscience research.
